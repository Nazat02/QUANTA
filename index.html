Three quantum principles
principle · i

Superposition

Between observations, every possible time exists simultaneously. The clock's particle cloud expresses this probabilistic fog — each point a candidate moment, each drift a flicker of possibility.

ψ = Σ αₙ|n⟩ · unobserved state
principle · ii

Observation

The act of looking is not passive. When you press Observe, you become the measurement apparatus. Your attention is the instrument that forces reality to commit to a single value.

⟨ψ|Ô|ψ⟩ · wavefunction collapse
principle · iii

Eigenstate

Collapse is irreversible. Three spikes emerge — hours, minutes, seconds — each a definite eigenvalue of the time operator. The system holds this state briefly before decoherence returns it to the fog.

Ô|ψ⟩ = λ|ψ⟩ · definite value

Visualization modes
Two ways to see

Choose your quantum model

QUANTA offers two renderings of the same underlying mathematics. Particle mode builds a diffuse probability cloud from individual points; wave mode expresses the same uncertainty as undulating interference patterns. Both collapse to the same answer.

particle
Particle Mode

Thousands of individual probability points drift in a bounded cloud. On observation, they contract to three luminous spikes, each tip labelled with its eigenvalue. Drag to rotate the disc in three-dimensional space.

wave
Wave Mode

Standing waves radiate from the disc's centre, their amplitudes encoding time. Collapse sends a ripple of constructive interference outward — the moment of measurement made visible as geometry.

auto
Watch Behaviour

Switch away and return. The act of navigating back counts as a fresh observation — the wavefunction collapses automatically the moment you look at the page, just as a glance at a watch would.

z-axis
Depth Control

Adjust the disc's tilt on the z-axis to move between a face-on view and full perspective projection. The three eigenstates become more spatially distinct at acute angles.
