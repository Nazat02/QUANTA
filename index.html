Three quantum principles
principle · i

Superposition

Between observations, every possible time exists simultaneously. The particle cloud expresses this probabilistic fog — each point a candidate moment, each drift a flicker of possibility.

ψ = Σ αₙ|n⟩ · unobserved state
principle · ii

Observation

The act of looking is not passive. When you press Observe, you become the measurement apparatus. Your attention is the instrument that forces reality to commit to a single value.

⟨ψ|Ô|ψ⟩ · wavefunction collapse
principle · iii

Eigenstate

Collapse is irreversible. Three spikes emerge — hours, minutes, seconds — each a definite eigenvalue of the time operator. The system holds this state briefly before decoherence returns it to the fog.

Ô|ψ⟩ = λ|ψ⟩ · definite value
How it works
01
Page loads in superposition

The moment you open QUANTA, thousands of particles drift across the disc in a probability cloud. No time is displayed. The system is in an indeterminate state — all hours, all minutes, all seconds at once.

02
Observation triggers collapse

Press Observe — or simply return to the tab after switching away. Your attention acts as the measurement event. The system reads the system clock at the exact moment of observation and begins to collapse.

03
Three eigenstates emerge

The particle cloud contracts into three luminous spikes along the disc — hours in violet-blue, minutes in cyan, seconds in ice-white. Each spike tip shows its exact value. Time is known.

04
Decoherence returns the fog

After a few seconds, the eigenstate decoheres. The spikes dissolve back into the probability cloud. Time becomes unknown again — until the next observation.

Visualization modes
Three ways to see

Choose your quantum model

QUANTA renders the same underlying mathematics three different ways. Each mode expresses the probability distribution of time differently — but all collapse to the same eigenstate when observed.

particle
Particle Mode

Thousands of individual probability points drift in a bounded cloud. On observation, they contract to three luminous spikes, each tip labelled with its eigenvalue. Drag to rotate the disc in 3D space.

gaussian
Gaussian Mode

A smooth probability density surface — the continuous cousin of particle mode. The bell curves encoding each time dimension overlap and shimmer in superposition before resolving to sharp peaks.

3d
3D Orbital Mode

A spherical s-orbital shell of particles surrounds a glowing nucleus. The three clock spikes extend outward through the shell in full perspective projection — time as spatial geometry.

auto
Watch Behaviour

Switching tabs counts as looking away. Returning counts as observation. The wavefunction collapses the moment you come back — like glancing at a watch on your wrist.

Technical specifications
4000+ particles

Individual probability points in the superposition cloud, each with independent drift velocity.

60 fps render

Smooth Canvas 2D animation loop, with requestAnimationFrame scheduling and offscreen buffering.

3 colour channels

Hours in violet-blue · minutes in cyan · seconds in ice-white. Each eigenstate visually distinct.

0 dependencies

Pure HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. No frameworks, no build step, no network requests after load.
